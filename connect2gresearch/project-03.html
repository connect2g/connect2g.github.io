Diversity: VR Body Transformation

Inspired by the work of Mel Slater of the University of Barcelona, I am working on a research project to study implicit bias through virtual body transformation. I am using Virtual and Augmented Reality as a tool to reduce the fear of the ‘other’ and confront our ‘in-group bias’. The project is being developed in response to the present climate of pervasive polarization and divisiveness in the United States.

Success of a society and any organization depends upon embracing diversity. However, research indicates that we are all living in silos and echo chambers in our real and online lives. We meet and communicate with individuals who are similar to us. We hardly seek interaction with people from different race, ethnicity, orientation, personality, etc. Lack of interaction with the ‘other’ makes us fear the ‘other’.

One of the easiest ways to promote diversity, equity, and inclusion is to eliminate the fear of the ‘other’. No matter our race, ethnicity, gender, orientation, religion, politics, fundamentally we are all the same human beings. Once we realize that, it is easy for us to come together.

In the real world, we form opinions about ourselves through bias informed through our own body and the social groups we associate with (racial, ethnic, gender, religious, sexual orientation, and others). We also imitate the behaviors of in-group members and conform to the in-group expectations. This imitation behavior is evident in our ‘in-group bias’ associated with interpersonal sensitivity and empathy. But Virtual Reality can have us identify these with a wide variety of avatars—people who don’t look and sound like us. Virtual Reality can help us free our body from the limitations of our minds.

This is an ongoing research project. Project outcomes include: creation of an interactive installation, and a game that aims to help the viewers confront their implicit bias.
