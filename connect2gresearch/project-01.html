Design for Social Impact

Sustaining marginalized traditional crafts and augmenting the income of craft workers living below the poverty line has been a passion of mine. I have worked as a design consultant on various projects funded by the United Nations. Currently I am working with artisan groups and craft based organizations in India, Peru, Kenya and China. I have designed contemporary market relevant products, website, packaging and social media campaigns for them.

I have presented my work in this area at selective and internationally prominent conferences like the AIGA Design Educators Conference. I am scheduled to present my research at the 11th Annual Design Principles & Practices Conference, Toronto, Canada in March 2017. I was awarded a Faculty Development Grant for my work in this area. In addition my paper titled “Social Media for Social Good” was published in the proceedings of INTED2012: 6th International Technology, Education and Development Conference, Valencia, Spain (ISBN: 978-84-615-5563-5, Pages: 3694-3697).

Recognizing the impact of my research I was invited to present at the Pontifical Catholic University of Peru (PUCP), Lima, Peru (August 2016); and Xiamen University, Fujian, China (December 2015). My proposal to conduct a workshop and work with the artisans was accepted by Kala Raksha, a grassroots social enterprise that is dedicated to the preservation of traditional crafts in India. Dates of these engagements are being finalized. I have also submitted papers/articles to the Design Principles and Practices: An International Journal; Iridescent, International Council of Graphic Design Associations (ICOGRADA) Journal of Design Research; and the Commemorative Book being published by the National Institute of Fashion Technology, New Delhi, India. All of these works are in the peer-review process. I remain committed to taking my work further in this area and explore ways to involve my students in it.
